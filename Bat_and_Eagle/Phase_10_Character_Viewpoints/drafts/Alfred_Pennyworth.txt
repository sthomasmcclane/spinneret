CHARACTER_VIEWPOINT_NAME: Alfred Pennyworth
It began, as so many things do in this city, with a new kind of darkness. Master Bruce, bless his stubborn heart, was already stretched thin, a solitary sentinel against Gotham's rot. Then came this 'Eagle One,' a whirlwind of lethal efficiency, carving a bloody path through the underworld. I saw the strain in Master Bruce's eyes, the way he wrestled with his code, a promise made to his parents that he would never break. My role, as ever, was to be his anchor, to remind him of his humanity, to offer a steady hand and a quiet word in the cavernous solitude of the Batcave. I recalled my own days in Her Majesty's Service, the 'necessary evils' that often led to unforeseen devastation. I couldn't let Bruce fall into that trap.

The city council, those opportunistic vultures, used the chaos to outlaw all masked heroes. It was a calculated move, orchestrated with the corrupt elements of the GCPD, as if they were waiting for a pretext. Master Bruce became a hunted man, his isolation deepening. While he was out there, a shadow among shadows, I kept the manor running, the Batcave humming, and the tea warm. I listened to his weary reports, tended to his wounds, and offered what wisdom I could, reminding him of his father's unwavering commitment to Gotham, a legacy of hope, not despair. When that skyscraper fell, a tragic monument to both Eagle One's recklessness and the city's deep-seated corruption, I saw the defeat in him. But I also saw the flicker of renewed resolve, a quiet determination to prove that his way, the humane way, was the only true path. My boy, always fighting for Gotham's soul.

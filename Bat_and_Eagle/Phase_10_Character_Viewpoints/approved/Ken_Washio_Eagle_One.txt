CHARACTER_VIEWPOINT_NAME: Ken Washio (Eagle One)
Gotham. A cesspool. My handlers were right; this city was ripe for cleansing. The local 'vigilante,' Batman, was a joke, clinging to a naive code while the criminals thrived. My training, honed since I was thirteen after my mother's death, taught me efficiency, taught me that true order requires decisive action. My bird-themed boomerangs, crafted with the unique metallurgy of my homeland, were instruments of justice. I systematically targeted the mid-level gang leaders, creating the necessary vacuum, destabilizing their pathetic power structures. The turf war was a predictable, and desired, outcome.

The money-laundering operation, protected by the corrupt GCPD, was a prime target. My assault was swift, lethal. Collateral damage is regrettable, but necessary. The city council's anti-vigilante legislation was a minor inconvenience, easily circumvented. Batman's attempts to confront me were futile; his 'non-lethal' approach was a weakness I exploited. I recognized some of his techniques, echoes of elite training, but he lacked the conviction to finish the fight. My handlers expressed 'disappointment' after the skyscraper incident – the explosion wasn't my doing, but it served its purpose, exposing the rot. I continued my mission, focused on dismantling the networks, restoring true order. My father's mysterious death, a shadow in my past, only reinforced my belief that weakness invites destruction. This city needed a firm hand, and I was that hand.

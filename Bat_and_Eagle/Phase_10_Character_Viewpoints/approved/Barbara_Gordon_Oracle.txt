CHARACTER_VIEWPOINT_NAME: Barbara Gordon (Oracle)
Gotham's always been a mess, a tangled web of corruption and desperation, but when Eagle One hit, it became a full-blown data nightmare. My screens lit up with the kind of lethal efficiency that made my old Batgirl days feel like a playground skirmish. This guy wasn't just a vigilante; he was a precision instrument, and his methods were tearing the city apart, pushing Bruce to the brink. From my hidden hub, the hum of servers my constant companion, I dove deep. Every movement, every kill, every piece of foreign tech on those bird-themed boomerangs – I dissected it all. My own paralysis, the moment I realized my mind was my most potent weapon, fueled my resolve. Bruce needed a non-lethal solution, and I was going to find it.

The anti-vigilante legislation was a predictable power play by the corrupt council and GCPD. They'd been planning it, just waiting for the right incident. It made Bruce a target, but it also made my role even more critical. While he was out there, a ghost in the machine, I was the eyes and ears, the strategic brain. I intercepted their comms, linking the GCPD captain to the syndicate, and when that data chip surfaced from the skyscraper's wreckage – a pre-planted device, not Eagle One's doing, I noted – I knew we had the leverage. The city was burning, but the truth, meticulously gathered and strategically leaked, was our only way out. It was a fight for information, and I was ready to win it.

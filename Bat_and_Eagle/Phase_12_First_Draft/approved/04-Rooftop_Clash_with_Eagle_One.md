## 04-Rooftop_Clash_with_Eagle_One

The rain fell. A steady, indifferent hiss on the rooftop gravel. It slicked the grotesque faces of the stone gargoyles and collected in pools around twisted rebar. Another shadow waited on the ledge, a stark shape against the bruised purple of the Gotham sky. White armor, segmented and sleek. A helmet sculpted into the sharp profile of a raptor. A short cape, split down the middle, fluttered in the wind like tail feathers. He stood perfectly still, a phantom in the downpour.

Batman landed without a sound, his own cape, heavy with rainwater, absorbing the impact. He was a piece of the night, a hulking silhouette of black and grey Kevlar plates. He moved first. No warning. A blur of motion intended to end the fight before it began, a strike aimed at the brachial nerve plexus. Disarm. Incapacitate. Question.

The white figure didn't flinch. He flowed. A pivot on the ball of his foot, impossibly fast, brought him inside the arc of the strike. A silver glint in his hand. It wasn't a dodge, it was an answer. A boomerang, shaped like a wing and sharpened to a razor’s edge, slashed through the air where Batman's throat had been a millisecond before. Batman contorted, the weapon’s edge slicing a thin, cold line across his cowl. It spun back, a silver arc in the night, and slapped neatly into its owner’s waiting palm.

The fight was on. A brutal ballet on the slick gravel. Batman’s fists were hammers, precise and powerful, aimed at joints, tendons, weak points in the armor. Each blow was met with a deflection, a parry, a counter that aimed for the kill. The man in white fought with an unholy grace, his movements economical, his bird-shaped blades a constant, whispering threat. One ricocheted off a steel conduit, sparking, forcing Batman to use his cape as a weighted shield. Another embedded itself in the concrete inches from his head.

They crashed across the roof, a trail of shattered masonry and twisted ventilation ducts in their wake. The white figure was relentless. He didn’t seem to need purchase. He leapt onto the parapet, balanced on the slick stone, then launched himself at the sheer brick wall of the adjoining building. His feet found impossible holds. He ran, horizontal to the ground, a gravity-defying sprint across the rain-soaked brick.

Batman fired his grapple. The hook bit into a cornice, pulling him skyward in a jarring ascent. He landed on the higher roof just as the white figure vaulted over the ledge. For a moment, they faced each other across a chasm of churning steam from a ruptured vent.

“真の秩序を取り戻す.” The voice was filtered, synthetic, but the Japanese was sharp. *Restore true order.*

Then the figure lunged. It wasn't a simple attack. It was a kata. A feint with the left hand, drawing the eye, while the body dropped into a low, coiled stance, channeling all force into a rising right-hand strike. A G-Force feint. Taught in only one place. Not to vigilantes. Not to criminals.

To operatives.

The world narrowed. This wasn't a madman. This was an asset. A weapon. And it was pointed at his city.

The white figure saw the flicker of recognition. He tossed a small pellet to the ground. It erupted not in smoke, but in a blinding magnesium flare that burned the night into a searing white negative. When Batman’s vision cleared, the man was gone. A fading silhouette leaping an impossible gap to the next building over, landing, and disappearing into the concrete maze.

Pursuit was suicide. A chase through streets filled with witnesses, an invitation for more destruction. A trap.

He let him go.

A single batarang left his glove. It didn’t fly at the fleeing figure. It spun low, clipping the back of his boot and tearing away a small shard of the white composite armor before clattering onto the gravel.

Batman retrieved the fragment. It was feather-light, impossibly strong. He stood alone in the storm, the hiss of the rain the only sound. Below, sirens began to converge, their wails climbing the walls of the skyscrapers. Another night of fear for Gotham. Another masked freakshow tearing up the skyline. He had failed to stop the killing. He had failed to make an arrest. But the mission had changed. He wasn't hunting a vigilante anymore. He was hunting a ghost.

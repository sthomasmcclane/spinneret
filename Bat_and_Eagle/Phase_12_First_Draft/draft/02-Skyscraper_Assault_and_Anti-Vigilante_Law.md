## 02-Skyscraper_Assault_and_Anti-Vigilante_Law

The rain came down in sheets, washing the filth of the city into gutters already choked with yesterday’s sins. It was a baptism that never took. From his perch on a stone gargoyle, Batman watched the Kord Tower. A monument of glass and steel that scraped the perpetually bruised sky. A beacon of corporate greed. Tonight, it was also a bank.

His cowl’s comm crackled to life. Oracle’s voice. No preamble. “Movement. Penthouse level. Not Maroni’s men.”

The lenses of his mask zoomed in, cutting through the deluge. A flicker of motion behind the tinted glass. A ghost. Something impossibly fast. Something white.

The penthouse windows exploded outward. A storm of tempered glass rained down on the streets below, unheard over the city’s drone. Before the last shard hit the pavement, the figure was inside.

Security feeds, hijacked and sanitized by Oracle, flooded Batman's display. A sterile lobby became a slaughterhouse. The figure was a blur of white composite armor, accented with crimson. A helmet molded into the merciless visage of a bird of prey. He didn’t stalk. He flowed. A whirlwind of precise, lethal motion.

Syndicate enforcers, hard men in cheap suits, raised their weapons. They never fired. Crescent-shaped objects, sharp and metallic, sliced through the air. They returned to a waiting gauntlet, their edges dripping. Men crumpled to the marble floor, arterial sprays painting the walls. It was efficient. Brutal. An equation of violence solved with swift, final subtractions.

Then came the cops. GCPD uniforms. Captain Brady’s hand-picked crew. They were just as surprised as the mobsters. Just as dead a moment later. A feathered boomerang, a grim parody of his own tool, embedded itself in one officer’s throat. Another spun through the air, catching a second officer across the chest, shearing through his vest like paper. The attacker didn't distinguish between the uniform and the suit. They were all just targets. Obstacles.

“Confirming,” Oracle’s voice was a flat line in his ear, a counterpoint to the carnage on his screen. “Intercepted Brady’s comms half an hour ago. He was guaranteeing Maroni a clear window.”

So the cops were dirty. No surprise. Gotham was a rot that went to the bone. But this… this was different. This wasn’t justice. It was extermination.

“Got a clean image of the weapon from a wall camera,” Oracle continued. “High-tensile steel. The alloy is… complex. Traces of unique folding techniques. Japanese.” A beat. “Shaped like a wing. A razor wing. I’m calling them Birdarangs.”

Batman’s jaw tightened. A low growl rumbled in his chest, lost to the storm. He didn’t give the name the dignity of a reply. He just watched.

Below him, the city watched, too. On every bar TV, every jumbo screen in the plazas, the live news feeds showed the aftermath. Helicopters swarmed the tower like flies on a corpse. Pundits screamed. Citizens texted their rage and their fear. Some called it savagery. Others, a long-overdue cleansing.

Then the feed cut away. An emergency broadcast. Councilman Travers, his face a mask of practiced outrage, stood at a podium. Words spilled from his mouth. Poison wrapped in civic duty. *Escalation. Uncontrolled menace. Our brave officers…*

The vote was unanimous. The Vigilante Enforcement Act. Effective immediately. All masked activities deemed a Class A felony. A warrant issued. For any and all.

The feed switched to a live shot of police headquarters. Two officers on the roof, unlocking the housing of the Bat-Signal. They heaved the great lamp onto its side. The light on the clouds, his light, sputtered and died.

The city had just declared war. On him.

He remained motionless, a part of the stone he knelt on. The rain washed over the hard lines of his armor, tracing paths down the scalloped points of his cape. He was an outlaw. A target. The man in white had made him a relic in a single night.

He stared down at the streets. His city. His burden.

Let them come.

## Scene 2: Skyscraper Assault and Anti-Vigilante Law

The Heavily Fortified Skyscraper, a monument to corporate hubris, gleamed with a false sense of invulnerability against the bruised Gotham night. Inside, a sophisticated money-laundering operation hummed, its illicit gears turning under the tacit protection of corrupt GCPD officers. Their uniforms, usually symbols of order, were now cloaks for complicity, their faces impassive as they guarded the ill-gotten gains.

Then, the whirlwind struck. Eagle One, a blur of lethal force, breached security with an almost supernatural ease. He moved through the gleaming corridors like a phantom, his 'Bird Run' allowing him to scale walls and ceilings, a terrifying avian predator in a concrete jungle. Bird-themed boomerangs, sharp and silent, flew with deadly precision, incapacitating and killing syndicate enforcers. The air filled with the sickening thud of bodies, the sharp crack of bone, and the metallic tang of fresh blood. Several high-ranking police officers, caught in the crossfire or directly involved in the scheme, also fell, their cries cut short by the unforgiving blades.

Chaos erupted, a symphony of screams and gunfire. News channels, ever hungry for spectacle, broadcast the devastation live, their cameras capturing the carnage in stark, unforgiving detail. Public opinion, already a fractured mirror, polarized instantly. Some lauded Eagle One's decisive, if brutal, action, seeing him as a necessary evil to cleanse the city's rot. Others condemned the escalating violence, fearing a descent into utter anarchy.

Miles away, in her hidden technological hub, Barbara Gordon, Oracle, watched the unfolding horror on her glowing screens. Her fingers flew across the keyboard, a silent dance of data analysis. She intercepted encrypted communications, a chilling exchange between a high-ranking GCPD captain and a crime syndicate boss, confirming their insidious alliance. "They're using this," the captain's voice, distorted but clear, crackled through her earpiece, "to push through the legislation. Blame the masks." Oracle's jaw tightened. She also analyzed a damaged boomerang, recovered from the scene by one of her anonymous contacts. Its unique metallurgic technique, pioneered in Japan, was unmistakable. "Birdarangs," she muttered, a dry, ironic humor in her voice, knowing Bruce would despise the moniker.

On a shadowed rooftop, Batman watched the news reports, the flickering images of destruction and public outrage reflected in the grim lines of his cowl. The corrupt city council, seizing on the public's fear and outrage, swiftly passed draconian anti-vigilante legislation. The words "outlawed," "felony," "capture on sight" echoed from the newscaster's voice. He was no longer a guardian, but a hunted figure, forced to operate entirely outside the law. The stakes for his every move had escalated dramatically. A single misstep, and Gotham would lose its last, desperate hope.

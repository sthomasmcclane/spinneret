## Scene 4: Rooftop Clash with Eagle One

The wind, a cold, biting whip, tore across Gotham's rooftops, snapping at their capes like angry spirits. Below, the city lights blurred into a chaotic tapestry, a stark contrast to the focused intensity of the two figures locked in a deadly ballet. Batman, guided by Oracle's precise intelligence, moved first, a shadow striking with calculated precision. His objective: disarm, incapacitate.

Eagle One, a dark, avian silhouette against the bruised sky, countered with raw, lethal force. His 'birdarangs,' a blur of glinting metal, sliced through the air, each one a promise of death. The battle raged across the shattered concrete and twisted metal of the rooftop, a destructive dance that left a trail of debris in its wake. Batman, a master of martial arts, moved with fluid grace, deflecting lethal blows, seeking openings for a non-lethal takedown. He parried a boomerang with his gauntlet, the clang echoing sharply in the night, then lunged, aiming for Eagle One's arm, a precise strike to disable.

But Eagle One was a force of nature, his movements impossibly fast. He used his "Bird Run," scaling a sheer wall with effortless agility, a human fly defying gravity. He launched himself back into the fray, a flurry of kicks and strikes, each one aimed to kill. "True order," Eagle One snarled, his voice a guttural rasp, "will be restored!" The words, spoken in rapid Japanese, were lost to the wind, but the intent was clear.

As Eagle One spun, launching another volley of boomerangs, Batman recognized a specific, highly advanced martial arts move, a technique he had only encountered during his own global training, taught exclusively to elite government operatives in a specific, clandestine region of Asia. A cold realization settled in his gut: this was no mere vigilante.

Despite Batman's strategic brilliance, his physical prowess, and Oracle's invaluable intel, Eagle One's ruthless efficiency and unexpected tactical advantages proved too much. He was a ghost, a whirlwind, always one step ahead. With a final, powerful kick that sent Batman skidding across the broken concrete, Eagle One launched himself into the night, disappearing into the labyrinthine shadows of Gotham's skyline.

The aftermath was immediate and devastating. The visible destruction, the shattered concrete, the twisted metal, would be broadcast across every news channel. The continued presence of the lethal vigilante, his escape a stark testament to his power, would fuel further public outcry and deepen the city's pervasive fear. Batman stood alone on the ravaged rooftop, the wind whipping his cape, the bitter taste of defeat in his mouth. He had failed to apprehend him. Gotham was still in danger.

## 01-Eagle_Ones_Violent_Arrival

Rain didn't fall on Gotham. It bled from a bruised sky, soaking the city in its own misery. From the shoulder of a stone gargoyle, Batman watched the infection fester.

Down below, in the throat of an alley slick with refuse and neon runoff, two crews faced off. The Maronis and the Falcone Juniors. Cheap muscle, cheaper guns. Posturing. Shouting. The air crackled with the kind of stupid that gets people killed. A stray civilian, a mother clutching a grocery bag, flattened herself against a grimy brick wall, face a mask of primal fear. Another night, another pointless turf squabble he’d have to break up. A few shattered bones. A lesson taught. It was his routine. His burden.

He shifted his weight, preparing to drop into the chaos.

Then, motion. A flicker in the periphery. Not his.

Something descended into the alley, landing without a sound. A figure in stark white armor, accented with crimson. A helmet shaped like a bird of prey, its impassive visor reflecting the guttering neon sign of a pawn shop. He stood in the alley's center, a ghost in the urban machine.

The shouting stopped. The guns, hesitant, turned on the newcomer.
"Who the hell are you?" one of the Maroni thugs spat.

The figure didn't answer. His arm snapped out. Something small and sharp sliced through the rain-soaked air with a vicious hum. A boomerang, shaped like a wing. It found the loudmouth's throat. Not to stun. To sever. The man collapsed, a gurgling fountain of dark red spilling onto the wet asphalt.

Panic erupted. A disease of frantic motion and blind firing. Muzzle flashes strobed against the wet walls. The white figure was a blur. Untouchable. He moved with a liquid economy Batman had never witnessed. Another winged blade flew, embedding itself in the chest of a Falcone gunman. A third took a man in the eye. They were not tools of intimidation. They were surgical instruments of death.

Batman remained frozen, a statue of disbelief. This wasn't a fight. It was a cull.

The man in the avian helmet closed the distance on the Maroni leader, a paunchy brute named Tiny Sal. Sal fumbled with his pistol, his face a mess of shock and terror. The white figure didn't even break stride. He vaulted over a dumpster, his movements unnaturally fluid, and drove a bladed weapon—another boomerang held like a dagger—deep into Sal’s sternum. He twisted it. A final, deliberate act of punctuation.

Sal slumped to the ground. Dead. The execution was cold. Efficient. Perfect.

The survivors broke. They dropped their weapons and scrambled over each other, rats fleeing a sinking ship. The white figure stood over his work, impassive. He methodically retrieved his bloody blades from the corpses. A hunter cleaning his kill.

Above, the chop of rotor blades beat against the downpour. News choppers. Their searchlights speared the darkness, pinning the brutal tableau in a harsh, unforgiving glare for the entire city to see.

The hunter looked up, straight into the lights, his visor hiding any expression. Then, with a burst of impossible speed, he scaled a fire escape and vanished into the city’s concrete canopy.

On his perch, Batman’s hands clenched into fists, the reinforced knuckles groaning. He had watched. Done nothing. The alley below was a slaughterhouse. The peace, fragile as it was, had been shattered. This wasn’t just a new player. It was a new plague. One that didn't just break bones, but ended lives without a second thought. And the city had just seen it happen, live and in color. The fire was lit. The gasoline was everywhere.

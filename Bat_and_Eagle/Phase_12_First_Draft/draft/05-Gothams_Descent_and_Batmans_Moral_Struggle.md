## 05-Gothams_Descent_and_Batmans_Moral_Struggle

The screens bled poison. Dozens of them, stacked in the cold dark, each one a window into a city tearing itself apart. The same lies echoed from every channel, a chorus of manufactured panic conducted by men in blue. A GCPD spokesman, a bulldog of a man with a cheap suit and an expensive lie, pointed a fat finger at the camera.

“…escalation caused by the Bat’s brand of chaotic vigilantism.”

Another screen. Flames chewing on the skeleton of a tenement building. A reporter, rain plastering her hair to her skull, clutched a microphone.

“…while others are calling the city’s newest protector a grim necessity. Decisive. Effective.”

Effective. The word hung in the damp cave air like cordite.

He turned from the light, letting the shadows swallow him whole. The armor felt heavy. Too heavy. Each plate a piece of a promise he wasn't sure he could keep. A promise the city no longer seemed to want.

A drop of water fell from a stalactite overhead. A hollow sound. It struck the stone floor and was gone. Another followed. A slow, merciless clock ticking toward failure. He paced the length of the platform, the hard soles of his boots scraping against the concrete. A caged animal. This whole damn cave was a cage.

His circuit brought him past the glass case. Inside, a Robin suit stood empty. A different failure. Beyond it, on a small, steel table, sat a framed photograph. Thomas and Martha Wayne, smiling under a sun that never shone on this city anymore. They looked happy. They looked alive.

He stopped. The memory wasn't a thought. It was a physical thing. The sudden chill of the night air. The glint of a cheap revolver. A string of pearls snapping, scattering like teeth on the wet pavement.

Was this working? All the broken bones, the nights without sleep, the blood he’d washed from his gloves. For what? For a city that cheered for a killer? A man in a bird costume who left bodies behind like breadcrumbs. Clean. Final.

The line blurred. A smudge of grey in the absolute black. It would be so easy. To cross it. Just once. To stop the infection at its source. No more revolving doors at Arkham. No more chances. An eye for an eye. Wasn't that justice?

He imagined it. The brutal efficiency. The satisfying crack of a neck. The end of the chase. A quiet city. A peaceful city.

A city built on a foundation of corpses.

He stood before the photograph, a ghost staring at ghosts. The man on the news called the killer decisive. They called Batman chaos. Maybe they were right. His way was messy. It was hard. It never ended.

He reached out, his gauntleted fingers hovering over the cold glass of the frame. He saw his mother's eyes. Not the dead, vacant things in the alley. The eyes in the picture. Full of a light he'd sworn to protect.

He closed his fist. A choice. Remain the symbol they scorned, or become the monster they craved. Fail his city, or fail his parents.

The monitors still flickered behind him, spewing their venom into the dark. He turned his back on the photograph. Walked to the main console. One by one, he killed the screens. The spokesman’s face dissolved into black. The reporter’s voice choked into silence. The fires vanished.

The cave fell into a profound, echoing quiet. Only the drip of water remained.

He stood in the absolute dark. The weight was still there. But it had shifted. It was no longer the crushing burden of doubt. It was the solid, familiar weight of resolve. Harder now. Colder. A purpose sharpened on the stone of his own soul. He would not break. He would not kill. He would show them all what justice really was.

## Scene 3: Batcave Introspection and Oracle's Counsel

The Batcave, a vast, echoing maw beneath Wayne Manor, hummed with the quiet power of advanced technology. Batman, weary and isolated, stood before the glowing Batcomputer screens, the blue light painting stark shadows on his grim face. Footage of Eagle One's latest rampage flickered across the monitors: bodies, chaos, the chilling efficiency of a predator. Public condemnation, a venomous chorus, weighed heavily on him, each headline a fresh wound. He felt the immense pressure, a suffocating weight, to compromise his non-lethal code, to meet fire with fire.

A vivid flashback, sharp as a shard of glass, pierced his thoughts. Years ago, during his rigorous global training, a grimy alley in a forgotten city. A criminal, broken and bleeding, at his mercy. His hand had tightened, the urge to end the suffering, to end the source of suffering, almost overwhelming. But then, the faces: Thomas and Martha Wayne, smiling, alive, their eyes full of love. The memory had pulled him back from the precipice, a cold splash of reality. He had not killed that man. He would not kill now.

A soft rustle of fabric, and Alfred Pennyworth approached, a steaming cup of Earl Grey in his steady hand. "Master Bruce," he said, his voice a calm counterpoint to the storm raging within Batman, "a moment, if you please." He placed the tea on a nearby console. "I recall a mission, during my less domestic days with Her Majesty's Secret Service. A 'necessary evil,' they called it. A targeted elimination, meant to prevent a greater catastrophe." Alfred's gaze met Bruce's, unwavering. "The catastrophe was indeed averted, but the ripples… the unforeseen devastation that followed, the innocent lives caught in the undertow of that 'necessary evil,' haunted me for years." His words, a subtle, paternal guidance, steered Bruce away from the seductive allure of lethal tactics.

Simultaneously, miles away in her hidden technological hub, Barbara Gordon, Oracle, was a whirlwind of focused energy. Her screens displayed intricate data streams, dissecting Eagle One's fighting style, predicting his movements, identifying potential vulnerabilities. Her fingers danced across the keyboard, a symphony of clicks and keystrokes. She recalled her own paralysis, the cold shock of the Joker's bullet, the moment her legs had failed her. But in that darkness, a new light had ignited: the realization that her intellect, her mind, was her greatest weapon. That pivotal moment strengthened her resolve now, fueling her meticulous search for a non-lethal solution for Batman.

Batman listened to Alfred's quiet wisdom, processed Oracle's relentless stream of data, and felt the internal storm begin to recede. The faces of his parents, the memory of his vow, Alfred's gentle counsel, Barbara's unwavering dedication to a better way – they coalesced into a singular, unyielding truth. He would not break his code. He reaffirmed his unwavering commitment, his resolve hardening like the ancient rock of the cave itself. He turned back to the Batcomputer, his mind already formulating a strategy, a complex dance of intelligence and precision, to neutralize Eagle One without shedding another drop of blood.

CHARACTER_NAME: Bruce Wayne / Batman
Layer One: A lone sentinel adhering strictly to his non-lethal code, constantly battling criminals and public suspicion.

Layer Two: breaking down the introduction, and expanding
Lone sentinel - How does this manifest? Batman operates in profound isolation, a self-imposed state driven by his mission and the need to protect his identity. While he has a support network in Alfred and Oracle, the burden of decision-making and the direct confrontation with Gotham's darkness are his alone. His isolation often leads to introspection and a heavy sense of responsibility, but rarely to a desire for conventional companionship, as he views it as a potential vulnerability.
Adhering strictly to his non-lethal code - Why is this code so important to him? This code is the bedrock of Batman's identity, a direct response to the senseless violence that orphaned him. It's a vow to never become the very evil he fights. Has it ever been tested? Constantly, especially in a city as brutal as Gotham, and most acutely with the arrival of Eagle One. What are the consequences of this code in a city like Gotham? It often puts him at a disadvantage against lethal foes, makes his work harder, and contributes to public frustration, as many believe more extreme measures are needed. How does he enforce it on himself? Through rigorous self-discipline, constant moral reflection, and the counsel of Alfred and Oracle, who serve as his ethical sounding boards.
Constantly battling criminals - What kind of criminals? From petty thugs to supervillains, but in this story, primarily the escalating gang conflicts fueled by Eagle One and the corrupt elements of the city council. Is it always physical confrontation, or does he use detective skills? Batman is first and foremost a detective; physical confrontation is often a last resort. He uses forensic analysis, surveillance, and psychological profiling to outmaneuver his adversaries. How does he stay ahead of them? Through meticulous planning, advanced technology, and an unparalleled understanding of Gotham's criminal underworld.
And public suspicion - How does this suspicion affect his work? It makes his operations more difficult, as citizens are less likely to cooperate, and authorities are more likely to pursue him. Does he care about public opinion? He cares about Gotham's well-being, which includes its faith in justice, but he understands that his methods are often misunderstood. He prioritizes saving the city over being liked by it. How does he deal with being misunderstood and feared by the very people he protects? With stoicism and a grim acceptance, believing that his actions will ultimately speak louder than public perception.

Layer Three: Basic Info and story role
Full Name: Bruce Wayne / Batman
Age: 40
What motivates this character? His unwavering commitment to justice, his non-lethal code, and his desire to protect Gotham from descending into anarchy, driven by the trauma of his parents' murder.
Single sentence summary of character's role in story: Batman is the protagonist who must uphold his moral code against a lethal new vigilante and expose corruption to save Gotham's soul.
Single paragraph summary of the character's role in story: Batman serves as the story's protagonist, a seasoned vigilante operating under a strict non-lethal code in a city that increasingly distrusts him. His role is to confront and neutralize Eagle One, a new, lethal vigilante whose actions threaten to plunge Gotham into chaos. Throughout the narrative, Batman must not only physically battle Eagle One but also internally grapple with the temptation to abandon his principles. Ultimately, he must expose the corrupt city council's manipulation and prove that his non-lethal approach is the true path to justice, restoring a glimmer of hope to Gotham.

## 08-Climactic_Showdown_and_Gothams_Healing

The rain fell like broken glass. It hammered the rooftop, a constant static of tiny impacts. Water bled down the black plates of his armor and tasted like copper in his mouth. Every joint was a grinding protest. Every breath, a debt.

Across the gravel, a figure in white. A porcelain shell trimmed in blood-red. The helmet, a raptor’s head, its visor a single crimson slit. He was a surgical tool in a city of rust and rot. Clean. Uncompromising.

A silver crescent whispered through the air. Not a tool for stunning. For breaking bones. This was an edge meant to sever. Batman moved, the cape a lead-weighted shadow, and the blade bit into the stone ledge behind him. A puff of concrete dust.

The white figure didn’t pause. A blur of motion. A spinning kick aimed for the throat. A stiff-fingered jab for the eye. Lethal geometry. No feints, no flourishes. Just points on a body where life could be ended.

Batman met the storm. His blocks were heavier. Sledgehammer blows against a scalpel. He absorbed the impacts, the shockwaves running up his arms. This wasn't a fight. It was containment. A cage of bruised knuckles and raw force built around a whirlwind.

The attacker used the city like a weapon. A sprint toward a sheer brick wall that didn’t end. His feet found purchase on the wet surface, defying gravity for three impossible steps. The 'Bird Run.' He launched from the wall, a second razor-edged boomerang already in his hand.

Batman took the blow head-on. A shudder rattled through his teeth. Below, the city screamed, a chorus of sirens that had lost their meaning. This thing in front of him was the source. The infection. It had to be cut out. No. Not cut out. Cauterized. The line was everything.

A spinning heel kick glanced off his cowl. The world dissolved into a smear of neon and static. He staggered. A breath too slow. A movement an inch too wide. A fatal error.

The man in white saw it. The red visor was a predator’s glare. He lunged. A committed, whirling assault. A pattern Oracle’s analysis had flagged from a grainy satellite feed. *Eagle Style, Form Seven: Tornado Talon.* High-risk. All-in. Predictable, if you were still standing to see it.

The intel scrolled behind Batman's eyes. Schematics. Psych evals. A ghost protocol from a place that wasn't on any map.

The opening was there. A sliver of time in the heart of the spin. A choice. Cripple him. A shattered knee. A permanent solution. Or something harder. Riskier.

He made it.

He didn't dodge. He moved inside the storm. He didn’t strike. He grabbed. One gauntlet clamped onto the attacking wrist, the other on the shoulder. He became a fulcrum, using the man’s own velocity against him. A twist. A sharp, brutal dislocation.

A wet pop echoed in the rain. A strangled noise, half-gasp, half-scream, was choked off by the helmet's modulator. The white figure crumpled, his arm hanging at an impossible angle. The silver boomerang clattered on the gravel. He twitched once, then lay still, defeated by pain alone.

Silence. Just the hiss of the downpour. Batman stood over him, chest heaving. He looked out at the city.

The GothCorp tower’s massive screen flickered. The looping news feed died. Replaced by lines of clean, white text. Bank transfers. Encrypted manifests. A photo of Travers shaking hands with a Falcone lieutenant. Oracle’s kill shot. Fired without a sound.

The riot sirens below began to change their rhythm. The chaotic swarm resolved into focused vectors. Toward City Hall. Toward precincts in the Bowery. The city was finally fighting the disease, not just the symptoms.

A new sound. Not a GCPD chopper. Lower. Quieter. A matte-black aircraft materialized from the storm clouds, hovering without a tremor. Ropes snaked down. Two figures in sterile tactical gear descended with inhuman grace. They didn't glance at Batman. They had eyes only for their broken asset.

Clipped, quiet Japanese cut through the rain. One knelt, deactivating the white suit. No recrimination. No surprise. A simple retrieval.

They secured Ken Washio to a harness and began their ascent. The aircraft melted back into the bruised sky. Batman was already gone. The rooftop was empty. Just another shadow claimed by the night.

The rain fell. It didn’t feel like glass anymore. It felt like water. It washed the grit from the stone, finding the cracks. The fever had broken. Now came the long, ugly work of scarring over.

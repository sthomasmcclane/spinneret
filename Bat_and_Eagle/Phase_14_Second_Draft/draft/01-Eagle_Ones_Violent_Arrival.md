## 01-Eagle_Ones_Violent_Arrival

The sky over Gotham bled. Rain slicked the alley below, turning neon signs into greasy smears on wet asphalt. Two crews, cheap muscle with cheaper guns, barked at each other across the narrow space. Posturing. A mother and child were pinned against a wall, caught in the crossfire of stupid.

A familiar weight settled in Batman’s gut. The city’s nightly sickness. He shifted on the gargoyle’s stone shoulder, muscles coiling. The calculus was simple. A few broken bones. Fear. Order restored. His routine.

A flicker. Something that didn’t belong.

A figure dropped into the alley's center. Silent. Stark white armor, etched with crimson lines. The helmet was sculpted into the features of a predator bird, its visor a black, impassive void. A ghost in the grime. The shouting died. Guns swiveled, hesitant.
"Who the hell are you?" a Maroni thug spat.

A cold spike of wrongness hit Batman. He froze, the impulse to drop turned to stone.

The figure in white didn't answer. An arm snapped out. A winged boomerang, honed to a razor edge, cut the air with a low hum. It bit deep into the talker’s throat. Not a warning. Not a wound. A final, severing strike. The man fell, a gurgling mess of red on the blacktop.

A knot of ice formed in Batman's stomach. His gauntlets creaked as his hands clenched.

Panic was a virus. It spread in a hail of frantic, useless gunfire. Muzzle flashes threw the scene into strobing relief. The white figure flowed through it. Untouched. There was no brute force. Just a lethal grace. Another winged blade flew, sinking into a man’s chest. A third took another through the eye. Clean. Surgical. Death delivered without passion. The white figure vaulted a dumpster, driving a blade held like a dagger into the last leader’s sternum. A deliberate twist. A full stop.

This wasn't a fight. It was a dissection.

The survivors broke. Dropped weapons. Scrambled away like the rats they were. The figure stood over his work. He retrieved his blades from the bodies, wiping them clean on a dead man’s coat.

The chop of rotor blades hammered down. News choppers. Their searchlights lanced through the rain, pinning the slaughter in a clean, white glare. For the whole city to see. The figure looked up, visor catching the light. Then he was gone, scaling a fire escape with an unnatural burst of speed, swallowed by the concrete canyons.

Batman remained on his perch. A stone sentinel that had done nothing. He had watched. The peace, his peace, was a lie. The alley was a tomb. A new player was on the board. One who didn't follow the rules. One who offered a faster, simpler solution. The city had just seen it. Live. In color. A new fire was lit. And Gotham was soaked in gasoline.

## 05-Gothams_Descent_and_Batmans_Moral_Struggle

The screens bled poison. Dozens of them, stacked in the cold dark. Windows into a city chewing on its own tail.

A GCPD spokesman. Bulldog face, cheap suit, expensive lie. His fat finger jabbed from the screen. “…escalation caused by the Bat’s brand of chaotic vigilantism.”

Another channel. A tenement skeleton, licked by flames. A reporter clutched her mic, rain plastering hair to her skull. “…calling the city’s newest protector a grim necessity. Decisive. Effective.”

Effective. The word coiled in his gut. A cold snake.

He turned from the light. Let the shadows of the cave swallow him. The weight on his shoulders wasn't just armor anymore. It was the city itself. A tombstone he carried on his back.

A drop of water hit stone. A hollow tick. Then another. A clock counting down to nothing.

He paced. The scrape of armored boots on rock echoed in the vast dark. A caged beast wearing the shape of a man. The cave wasn't a sanctuary. It was a hole to hide in.

His circuit took him past the glass. A boy’s uniform, frozen in silent accusation. A different kind of failure. Beyond it, a small steel table. A photograph. Thomas and Martha Wayne. Smiling under a sun that had set for good.

He stopped. The memory hit him. Not a thought. A physical shock. The cold of the alley air. Gunmetal glinting under a streetlamp. The snap of a pearl necklace. White beads skittering across wet pavement like scattered teeth.

Was any of it working? The broken bones. The blood he washed from his gauntlets night after night. All for a city that now cheered for a killer. A man who left clean bodies. Final solutions. No revolving doors.

The line wasn’t a line anymore. Just a smudge in the dark.

It would be easy. To cross it. To be the surgeon the city seemed to want. Cut out the rot. One clean snap of a neck. One final, satisfying crack. No more chase. No more games. A quiet city. A peaceful city.

A city built on a foundation of corpses. His corpses.

He stood before the photograph, a ghost staring at ghosts. They called the other man decisive. They called him chaos. Maybe they were right. His way was a mess. A war of attrition without end.

His gauntlet hovered over the cold glass of the frame. He saw his mother’s eyes. Not the empty things he remembered from the alley. The eyes in the picture. Alive.

He made a fist. The hydraulics in the glove whined softly. A choice. Be the monster they craved, or the symbol they hated.

He turned his back on the smiling ghosts. Stalked to the main console. One by one, he killed the screens. The spokesman’s lie dissolved to black. The reporter’s praise choked into silence. The fires went out.

The cave fell into a profound, echoing quiet.

Only the drip of water remained. Louder now. A steady, rhythmic torment.

He stood in the absolute dark. The weight was still there. It hadn’t shifted. It had settled. Hardened. A purpose sharpened on the unforgiving stone of his own soul. He would not break. He would not kill.

He would show them what justice was. Even if he had to carve the lesson into their bones.

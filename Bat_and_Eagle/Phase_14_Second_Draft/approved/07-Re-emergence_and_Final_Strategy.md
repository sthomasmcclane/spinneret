## 07-Re-emergence_and_Final_Strategy

The drip. A steady, rhythmic torment somewhere in the dark. A water-clock counting down to nothing. It echoed the city’s endless rain, a misery that seeped through a thousand feet of rock. That sound, and the low hum of the servers. Nothing else.

He stood before the monitor. A monolith of black armor and bruised flesh. The cowl sat on the console, a hollowed-out piece of the night. Beside it, a cup of tea had gone cold. He hadn’t been sleeping. He’d been simmering. The beast was awake.

Alfred’s footsteps were a soft defiance on the cold floor. He set a fresh cup next to the old one. The steam rose from it, a fragile ghost fighting the cavern’s chill.

“Your father,” Alfred said, his voice cutting through the hum, “once faced a hostile takeover. Men he trusted meant to gut the company for parts.”

Bruce’s eyes stayed locked on the screen. A schematic of a city tearing itself apart.

“He didn’t fight them in the boardroom,” Alfred continued. “He went to the factory floor. Spoke to the workers. Told them the truth. He trusted them to see it.” A pause. “He lost a fortune that quarter. He saved the company.”

A history lesson. A reminder of the foundation. Bruce’s jaw was a hard line of stone.

A chime shattered the quiet. The city map vanished. A stylized eye blinked open. Oracle.

“The chip is clean,” Barbara’s voice said, steel wire through the vocoder. “Encrypted comms, offshore accounts, kill orders. It ties half of GCPD command to Falcone’s old network.”

Data scrolled past. A web of rot. On a smaller screen, silent security footage looped. The figure in white and red and blue. A helmet styled like a raptor’s head. He ran straight up a glass tower, defying gravity. The video froze. A close-up on a bladed boomerang, shaped like a feather. A killing tool. Simple. Clean.

Bruce glanced at a display case. At the blunted, bat-shaped steel within. Tools of concussion. Instruments of capture. Not evisceration.

“His tactics are textbook wet-work,” Oracle said. “Government-sanctioned.”

“Whose?” The word was a low growl of rust.

“Signal’s ghosted. But I found a misdirected packet. Buried deep in a Japanese intelligence server. Not a name. A designation.”

The footage was gone. Replaced by a single file. A single line of text glowed in the dark.

EAGLE ONE.

The name landed in the silence. The finality of a closing tomb. A soldier, not a madman. A professional. It changed the math.

“Two targets, Bruce,” Oracle stated, her logic cold. “The system, and the asset sent to burn it down. Leak the data, the rats go to ground. He becomes a ghost. Go after him, the city burns while the politicians scream for your head.”

A choice. Let the city fester, or let the killer escape. The only kind of choice Gotham ever offered.

He turned from the console. Walked past racks of his own trade. Smoke capsules. Bolas. Taser-tipped grapnels. Tools for a war of attrition. Not annihilation.

He stopped at the armor stand. Picked up the cowl. The pointed ears cut a familiar silhouette against the gloom.

“We don’t choose,” he said. The words were gravel. A promise. “We do both.”

He turned back to the screen. To Oracle.

“The leak needs a single, trusted voice. Find a journalist they can’t buy or bury.”

“Vicki Vale. I’m already in her system.”

“Good. When I make my move, you give her everything. All at once. A fire they can’t put out.”

He raised the cowl.

“Where?” Oracle asked.

“Tricorner Yards. It’s isolated. He favors high ground.” A chessboard of rusted gantries. “Leak a rumor. Falcone’s lieutenants are meeting to fill the vacuum. He won’t resist.”

A trap for a hawk. Baited with carrion.

He pulled the cowl over his head. The world narrowed to the white lenses of the mask. The hiss of the suit’s seal was the last quiet thing he heard. Then the roar of the engine tore through the cave, a promise of violence aimed at the heart of the storm. The plan was in motion. He was the plan.

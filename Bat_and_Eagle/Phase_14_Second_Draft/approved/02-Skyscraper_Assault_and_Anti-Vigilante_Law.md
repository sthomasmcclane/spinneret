## 02-Skyscraper_Assault_and_Anti-Vigilante_Law

The rain came down in sheets, a baptism that never took. It washed the filth of the city into gutters already choked with yesterday’s sins. From a stone gargoyle, Batman watched the Kord Tower scrape the perpetually bruised sky. A glass and steel monument to greed. Tonight, also a bank.

A crackle in his cowl. Oracle’s voice, stripped of warmth. “Movement. Penthouse. Not Maroni’s.”

*Action.*

The lenses of his mask zoomed, slicing through the deluge. Behind tinted glass, a flicker. A ghost in white.

Then the penthouse windows blew out. A storm of tempered glass rained down on the street, the sound swallowed by the city’s drone and the hammering rain. Before the last shard hit the pavement, a figure was inside.

*Action.*

The feeds came, sanitized by Oracle. A sterile corporate lobby. A flash of white composite armor, streaked with crimson. A helmet molded into the merciless face of a raptor. The figure didn’t stalk. It flowed. A liquid economy of motion. Syndicate enforcers in cheap suits raised their hardware. They never got a shot off. Crescent blades sliced the air, returning to a waiting gauntlet, their edges wet. Men dropped. Arterial sprays painted the marble red. A problem solved with finality.

*Reaction.*

A low growl rumbled in Batman's chest, lost to the storm. Cold. No wasted energy. No theatrics. Just subtractions.

*Action.*

Then the blue uniforms. GCPD. Captain Brady’s hand-picked crew. They were just as surprised. Just as dead a moment later. A feathered boomerang buried itself in one officer’s throat. A second spun through the air, shearing through another’s vest like it was paper. The attacker made no distinction. Uniform or suit. They were all obstacles.

*Reaction.*

“Confirming.” Oracle’s voice was a flat line against the carnage. “Brady’s comms from thirty minutes ago. Guaranteed Maroni a clear window.”

So the cops were dirty. Gotham was a rot that went to the bone. Old news. But this… this wasn't justice. It was an audit. Conducted with a blade.

*Action.*

“Clean image of the weapon,” Oracle continued. “High-tensile steel. Complex alloy. Traces of a Japanese folding technique.” A pause. “Shaped like a wing.”

*Reaction.*

Batman’s jaw tightened. He offered no reply. He just watched.

*Action.*

Below, the city watched, too. Every bar TV and plaza jumbo-screen bled with the live feeds. Helicopters swarmed the tower, flies on a fresh corpse. And then, an emergency broadcast. Councilman Travers. His face a mask of practiced outrage. Poison wrapped in civic duty. *Escalation. Uncontrolled menace. Our brave officers…* The vote was unanimous. The Vigilante Enforcement Act. Effective immediately. A warrant for any and all. The feed cut to GCPD headquarters. Two cops on the roof. They heaved the great lamp onto its side. The light on the clouds—his light—sputtered and died.

*Reaction.*

He remained motionless. A part of the stone. The rain washed over the hard lines of his armor, tracing paths down the scalloped points of his cape. The city had just declared war on him because of a killer who did the job better. The man in white had made him a relic in a single night. An outlaw. A target.

He stared down at the streets. His city. His burden.

Let them come.

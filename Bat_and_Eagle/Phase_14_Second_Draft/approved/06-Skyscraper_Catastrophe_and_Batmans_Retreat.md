## 06-Skyscraper_Catastrophe_and_Batmans_Retreat

The rain was a fist, hammering the rock above the cave. A constant, drumming beat for the city’s slow death. The chill inside wasn’t just the damp. It was the absolute zero of a tomb.

“He’s at Kajiwara Tower.” Oracle’s voice, stripped of all warmth. Pure signal.

The main monitor flickered to life. A figure in bone-white armor moved through a penthouse drenched in rain and neon. The helmet was sculpted, avian. A short, feathered cape, clean as fresh snow, snapped in the gale from a shattered window. The brightness of him was a knife in the eye. An obscenity in Gotham’s grime.

Batman was already in the Tumbler. Systems awoke around him with a low hum. His hands, tight in their gauntlets, knew their purpose. Intercept. Subdue. Contain.

On the screen, the white ghost didn't brawl. He culled. He flowed through panicked security guards, their pistols spitting uselessly into the storm. His weapons were shaped like the wings of a hunting bird, razor-thin. They left his hands, sliced through flesh and bone, and returned. Dripping. Men crumpled. Finality in every movement.

“He’s not fighting them,” Batman’s voice was a low growl, vibrating through the cockpit. “He’s erasing them.”

“Lethal intent confirmed,” Oracle said. “He’s working his way down. A methodical purge.”

The Tumbler’s engine coughed, then roared. A caged animal smelling blood. The cave exit ground open, a stone jaw preparing to spit him into the deluge. He had to stop it.

Then, a change in Oracle’s transmission. Not static. A subtle intake of breath. The sound of an anomaly. “Wait. Hold.”

The image on the screen froze, then spooled back. The white figure crashed through a window on the 48th floor, a line paying out as he dropped down the building’s glass face. A phantom swallowed by the storm. He was gone.

“He broke off,” Batman stated. A question hanging unspoken in the air.

“He did,” Oracle said. Her voice was tight. “Look at his egress point. Support column, office 48-C.”

She isolated the frame. An empty office. And in the corner, a flicker. A small, blinking red light on a package the white-clad killer had run right past. It wasn't his.

“A secondary device…”

The world on the screen became pure white light. The audio feed screamed, then died. The needles on the cave’s seismographs danced a frantic jig. A deep, hollow tremor rolled through the concrete floor, a ghost of the distant shockwave finding him in the dark.

Every feed exploded at once. News choppers swarmed a pillar of black smoke where a skyscraper used to be. The top twenty floors were just… gone. A wound in the sky, bleeding fire and steel onto the streets.

“Structural collapse,” Oracle’s voice was a ghost in the static. “God, Bruce. The collateral…”

A GCPD spokesman was on a live feed before the dust had settled. A man with a wet mustache and terrified eyes, a script shaking in his hand. He said ‘vigilante terrorism.’ He mentioned a war between masked freaks. He used Batman's name.

The lie was on the air before the first scream had died.

He saw the path laid out. The fear, already a cancer in this city, metastasizing. The anger, looking for a target. They had been given one. Him.

He could go out there. Another shadow in the smoke. A target for panicked cops and every punk with a gun. His presence wouldn't quell the chaos. It would feed it. Gasoline on the fire.

Or he could vanish.

Let them have their monster. Let the city choke on its own bile. A best bad choice. The only choice left on the board.

The engine died with a final, shuddering cough. He killed the lights. The monitors winked out, one by one.

“Oracle. Go dark,” he commanded. The words were stones dropped into a deep well. “All channels. Now.”

The connection severed.

He stepped out of the Tumbler into a profound, oppressive blackness. The distant, mournful wail of sirens was the only sound. A dirge for the city. A verdict on his failure. He stood in the dark, the weight of a falling skyscraper pressing down on him, a statue in a tomb of his own making.

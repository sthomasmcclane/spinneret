## 04-Rooftop_Clash_with_Eagle_One

The rain fell. A steady, indifferent hiss on the rooftop gravel. It slicked the stone gargoyles and pooled around twisted rebar. Another shadow waited on the ledge, a stark shape against the bruised purple of the Gotham sky. White armor, segmented and sleek. A helmet sculpted into the sharp profile of a raptor. A short, split cape fluttered like tail feathers. A phantom in the downpour.

Batman landed without a sound. His cape, heavy with rainwater, swallowed the impact. A piece of the night. Black and grey Kevlar plates. He moved first. No warning. A blur aimed at the brachial plexus. Disarm. Incapacitate. Question.

The white figure didn't flinch. He flowed. A pivot on the ball of his foot, bringing him inside the strike’s arc. A silver glint in his hand. Not a dodge. An answer. A boomerang, shaped like a wing and sharpened to a killing edge, slashed the air where Batman's throat had been a millisecond before. Batman contorted, the weapon slicing a cold line across the cowl’s armored neck. It spun back, a silver arc in the night, and slapped neatly into its owner’s palm.

The fight was on. Batman’s fists were hammers. Precise. Powerful. Aimed at joints, tendons, weak points in the armor. Each blow met a deflection. A parry. A counter that aimed for the kill. The man in white fought with a liquid economy, his bird-shaped blades a constant, whispering threat. One ricocheted off a steel conduit, sparking. Batman threw his cape, a weighted shield. Another blade embedded itself in concrete, inches from his head.

They crashed across the roof. A trail of shattered masonry. Twisted ventilation ducts. The man in white seemed to need no purchase. He launched himself at the sheer brick wall of the adjoining building. His feet found holds where there were none. A gravity-defying sprint across the rain-soaked brick.

Batman fired his grapple. The hook bit into a cornice, pulling him skyward. He landed on the higher roof just as the white figure vaulted over the ledge. Steam from a ruptured vent churned between them.

“真の秩序を取り戻す.” The voice was filtered, synthetic. The Japanese was sharp. *Restore true order.*

The figure lunged. Not an attack. A kata. A feint with the left hand, the body dropping low, coiling all force into a rising right-hand strike. G-Force feint. Taught in one place. Not to vigilantes. To operatives.

The world narrowed. A cold certainty settled in his gut. This wasn't a madman. This was an asset. A weapon.

The white figure saw the recognition in his stance. He tossed a pellet to the ground. It erupted. Not smoke. A blinding magnesium flare. The night burned into a searing white negative. When Batman’s vision cleared, the man was gone. A fading silhouette leaping an impossible gap, disappearing into the concrete maze.

A chase was a trap. An invitation. He let him go.

One batarang left his glove. It didn’t fly at the fleeing figure. It spun low, clipping the back of his boot. A small shard of white composite armor tore away, clattering onto the gravel.

Batman retrieved the fragment. Feather-light. Impossibly strong. He stood alone in the storm, the hiss of the rain the only sound. Below, sirens began to converge. Their wails climbed the walls of the skyscrapers. Another masked freakshow tearing up the skyline. He had failed. Failed to stop him. Failed to make an arrest. But the mission had changed. He wasn't hunting a vigilante anymore. He was hunting a ghost.

CHARACTER_NAME: Hal Jordan (Green Lantern)
### Layer One: The gameshow introduction.
A cocky, high-flying test pilot with a ring that manifests anything he can imagine, provided he has the will to spark it.

### Layer Two: breaking down the introduction, and expanding.
**Cocky test pilot:** How does it manifest? He is reckless, impulsive, and has a "need for speed" that often gets him into trouble. He relies on his instincts rather than plans and has a dry, snarky sense of humor that masks a deep fear of failure.
**Willpower:** Why is this his defining trait? He was chosen by the ring specifically for his ability to overcome great fear. This manifest as a stubborn refusal to give up, even when faced with cosmic-level odds. 
**Imagination:** How does this show? His constructs are often aviation-themed—jets, gatling guns, and turbines. He views the universe through the lens of a pilot, always looking for the "angle of attack."
**Reckless and Impulsive:** How does it affect him? He frequently clashes with Batman’s rigid plans. His partnership with Cosmos is hilariously dysfunctional; Hal wants to "fly" the UFO like a jet, while Cosmos is busy trying to catalog human radio waves.

### Layer Three: Basic Info and story role.
*   **Full Name:** Hal Jordan
*   **Age:** Mid 30s
*   **What motivates this character?** A desire to prove he's the best pilot in the galaxy and a sense of duty to the Green Lantern Corps.
*   **Single sentence summary of character's role in story:** Hal Jordan provides long-range orbital reconnaissance and space-faring artillery alongside Cosmos.
*   **Single paragraph summary of the character's role in story:** Hal acts as the League’s "eye in the sky." While the other heroes are grounded in cities, Hal and Cosmos are patrolling the atmosphere and the moon, hunting for Decepticon reinforcements. Hal’s role is to keep the "big picture" in check, using his ring to augment Cosmos's saucer-shape with hard-light engines. Despite their bickering, Hal relies on Cosmos’s deep-space sensors to track the Joker’s movements from orbit, making them the ultimate early-warning system for the team.

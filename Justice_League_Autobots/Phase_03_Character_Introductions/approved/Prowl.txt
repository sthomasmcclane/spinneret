CHARACTER_NAME: Prowl
### Layer One: The gameshow introduction.
A cold, logic-driven Autobot military strategist who views the war as a series of mathematical equations to be solved.

### Layer Two: breaking down the introduction, and expanding.
**Cold Logic:** How does it manifest? He speaks in a flat, clinical tone and often dismisses "emotional" arguments. He is willing to sacrifice pieces on the board to win the game, which causes tension with more compassionate heroes.
**Military Strategist:** Why is he so rigid? On Cybertron, he was the head of tactical security. He believes that order is the only thing standing between civilization and extinction. He is obsessed with rules and "proper procedure."
**Rezvani 'Dark Knight' Chassis:** How does this show? His vehicle mode is a black, armored fortress. It reflects his personality: impenetrable, tactical, and built for urban pacification.
**Logic-driven:** How does it affect him? He is the only Autobot who truly understands Batman. They share a "language of contingency." Prowl provides Bruce with the raw computing power to simulate millions of battle outcomes, making them the most dangerous tactical pair in the alliance.

### Layer Three: Basic Info and story role.
*   **Designation:** Prowl
*   **Rank:** Military Strategist / Second-in-Command
*   **What motivates this character?** The total cessation of the Decepticon threat through the most efficient means possible.
*   **Single sentence summary of character's role in story:** Prowl serves as Batman’s mechanical counterpart and tactical processor, managing the "ground-game" logistics of the war.
*   **Single paragraph summary of the character's role in story:** Prowl is the "Brain" of the Autobot resistance. He spends the majority of the story inside the Batcave, linked to the Bat-Computer, creating a global defense grid. He is the one who discovers the Joker's frequency "leak" in Megatron’s Fusion Cannon, allowing Batman to devise the final plan. His role is to be the "unemotional" voice that keeps the League focused on the mission, often forcing Batman to confront the hard logic of a war against sentient machines.

CHARACTER_NAME: Booster Gold
### Layer One: The gameshow introduction.
A fame-hungry time-traveler from the future with a heart of gold, trying to hide his "fraud" status by becoming the world's greatest celebrity hero.

### Layer Two: breaking down the introduction, and expanding.
**Fame-hungry:** How does it manifest? He is constantly looking for cameras, worrying about his "brand," and seeking sponsorships. He uses Skeets (his robot companion) to record his every move for future "historical records."
**Fraud status:** Why is he here? A failed athlete from the 25th century, he stole museum tech to travel back and play the hero. This creates a deep-seated insecurity—he feels like he doesn't belong with legends like Superman or Batman.
**Heart of gold:** How does this show? When the chips are down, he always does the right thing, even if it hurts his reputation. He is surprisingly brave when no one is watching.
**Celebrity Hero:** How does it affect him? He is the perfect partner for Jazz. Together, they handle the "PR" of the war, keeping the public calm with flashy displays and "heroic" poses, while secretly acting as a highly effective distraction unit.

### Layer Three: Basic Info and story role.
*   **Full Name:** Michael Jon Carter
*   **Age:** Late 20s
*   **What motivates this character?** Redemption for his past failures and a genuine, albeit ego-driven, desire to be remembered as a hero.
*   **Single sentence summary of character's role in story:** Booster Gold acts as the League’s flashy "Distraction and PR" unit, using Jazz to draw enemy fire with style.
*   **Single paragraph summary of the character's role in story:** Booster and Jazz are the team's "Hype Men." During the invasion of Metropolis, they lead the Decepticons on high-speed chases through the streets, turning the battle into a televised spectacle that draws the "Dreads" away from civilian centers. Booster's future-tech provides a unique advantage, as he can sometimes predict Decepticon movements before they happen. While the world sees him as a joke, his and Jazz's ability to manipulate the "narrative" of the war is what prevents the public from falling into a state of total anarchy.

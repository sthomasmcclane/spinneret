After the crushing defeat of the Rebellion's initial hopes, Leia Organa finds a perverse sense of purpose and power alongside her father, Darth Vader, as the remnants of the Rebel Alliance struggle against her ruthless efficiency and strategic genius, forcing them to confront the horrifying truth that some falls are permanent, and that true redemption may be an illusion for those who find peace in shadow.

Leia's Unique connection to the Force, once turned, could manifest in ways distinct from Vader's. Perhaps she excels in Force persuasion, manipulation of emotions, or strategic foresight, reflecting her political acumen and empathetic nature, now twisted for malevolent purposes. This would make her a unique and terrifying antagonist.

Vader's Unexpected Humanity - In the process of turning Leia, Vader experiences a flicker of his former self, a moment of regret and parental grief for his daughter and the path he's put her on, that he starts to doubt his own committment to the Dark Side, and seeks out Luke who, as in the original timeline, turn him back to the Light. They become a new Jedi duo against Palpatine and his new apprentice Leia, Darth Vexis.
